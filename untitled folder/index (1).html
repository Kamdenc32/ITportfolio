IT Support Quick Help

Simple solutions for common tech problems.

(Project for CoreTech IT Specialist Mock Interview)

Select an Issue Below:

  1. Check Wi-Fi Switch: Make sure Wi-Fi is turned on (look for a switch on laptops or a setting).
  2. Correct Network/Password: Double-check you're choosing the right network name and typing the password carefully.
  3. Restart Device: Turn your computer or phone off and on again.
  4. Restart Router: Unplug your Wi-Fi router, wait 30 seconds, plug it back in. Wait a few minutes for it to fully start.
  5. Run Troubleshooter: Use the built-in network help tool on your computer (Search for "Troubleshoot network").
  6. Check Other Devices: Can other phones/computers connect? If yes, the problem is likely your device. If no, it might be the router or internet service.
  7. Update Driver (If needed): If only your device has issues, its network software (driver) might need updating.
  1. Check Power & Cables: Is the printer turned on? Are the cables plugged in securely?
  2. Check Printer Display: Look for any error messages or blinking lights on the printer itself.
  3. Check Paper & Ink: Make sure there's paper loaded and ink/toner isn't empty.
  4. Correct Printer Selected: Did you choose the right printer when you clicked "Print"?
  5. Clear Print Queue: Sometimes old print jobs get stuck. Search for "Printers" in settings, find your printer, open the queue, and cancel all documents.
  6. Restart Computer & Printer: Turn both off, wait 10 seconds, then turn them back on (printer first).
  7. Network Printer Check: If it's a network printer, ensure it's connected to the network (check its connection status).
  8. Reinstall Driver (If needed): The printer's software (driver) might need to be reinstalled.
  1. Restart Your Computer: This is often the quickest fix for temporary slowdowns.
  2. Close Unused Programs: Too many apps open at once use up resources. Close ones you aren't using.
  3. Check Task Manager: Press `Ctrl+Shift+Esc` (Windows) or use Activity Monitor (Mac) to see if any program is using too much CPU or Memory. Close it if possible.
  4. Check Disk Space: If your main hard drive (usually C:) is almost full, it can slow things down. Delete unnecessary files or move them elsewhere.
  5. Run Virus Scan: Malware can significantly slow down your computer. Run a full scan with your antivirus software.
  6. Limit Startup Programs: Too many programs starting automatically when you turn on your computer can cause slowness. Check Task Manager (Startup tab) or System Settings to disable unnecessary ones.
  7. Check for Updates: Ensure your operating system (Windows/macOS) and software are up-to-date.

Password resets often require help from IT, especially for work accounts. Here's the general idea:

  • Look for a "Forgot Password" Link: On the login screen, there might be a link to reset it yourself (often requires email or security questions).
  • Contact IT Help Desk: If it's a work account (like a Windows login) or you can't reset it yourself, you'll need to contact your IT department.
  • Verification: Be prepared to verify your identity so IT knows it's really you.
  • Temporary Password: IT will likely give you a temporary password and require you to change it immediately after logging in.
